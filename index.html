About Muar District

Muar, known as the "Royal Town" of Johor, is a historic district blessed with rich culture and natural beauty. However, its strategic location along the Muar River and coastal areas makes it particularly vulnerable to flooding.

Our drainage management system serves over 330,000 residents across the district, monitoring critical infrastructure including river drainage, urban stormwater systems, and coastal flood defenses.

Understanding Flood Risks in Muar

Knowledge is our best defense against flooding

Flood-Prone Areas

  • Low-lying residential areas near Muar River
  • Commercial districts during high tide
  • Agricultural areas during monsoon season

Monsoon Season Impact

From November to March, Muar experiences increased rainfall that can overwhelm drainage systems. Our monitoring helps predict and respond to potential flooding events.

Early Warning Benefits

  • Faster emergency response
  • Better evacuation planning
  • Reduced property damage
  • Improved community safety

How You Can Help

Report any flooding, blocked drains, or unusual water accumulation immediately. Your timely reports help us maintain effective drainage systems for everyone.
